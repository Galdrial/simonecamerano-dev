About Me

I haven’t always worked in IT, but my passion for technology pushed me to change direction.
Today, I enthusiastically dedicate myself to full stack development, learning something new every day.
I believe in the value of curiosity, perseverance, and the power of reinventing oneself: my journey proves it’s never too late to follow your passions and build new skills.

My Story

How I became a developer (Spoiler: It wasn’t planned)

I’ll be honest: I didn’t wake up at ten years old knowing I’d become a web developer.

My journey started with a broken website. I was trying to launch a small project (which, by the way, never took off), and the template I bought was a mess.
Instead of giving up, I opened the code. I had no idea what I was looking at, but something about those colorful lines intrigued me.

One YouTube tutorial led to another. One night of “just one more tweak” became dozens. Before I knew it, I wasn’t just fixing that site—I was rebuilding it from scratch. And I loved every minute, between frustration and excitement.

What won me over wasn’t just solving problems (though I love a good debugging session at 2 a.m.). It was the creative power. With code, I could turn abstract ideas into real, functional experiences. I could create a button that surprises users. I could optimize loading times and literally save people time. I could build something that didn’t exist yesterday.

Today, I’m a full-stack developer who combines design and functionality. I write clean code that makes sense to people, not just machines. I believe the best solutions are invisible—users shouldn’t notice the technology, just enjoy the experience.

Why do I do it? Because every project is a new challenge, every bug is a puzzle, and every deploy is that moment of “wow, I really built this.”

Technology changes every day, and that’s exactly why I’m here—I love learning, adapting, and building things that matter.

Values & Work Philosophy

How I Work (And Why It Matters)

I have a simple philosophy: build things that don't suck.

Sounds basic, right? But here's what that actually means in practice:

QUALITY ISN'T NEGOTIABLE
I don't ship half-baked solutions. If I'm going to put my name on something, it needs to work—really work. Not "works on my machine" work, but "tested, optimized, and bulletproof" work. I'd rather take an extra day to do it right than spend weeks fixing it later.

USERS ARE HUMANS, NOT DATA POINTS
Every button I code, every form I design, there's a real person on the other side.
Maybe they're tired, maybe they're in a hurry, maybe they're using a phone with one hand while holding coffee. I build for that reality, not for perfect conditions that don't exist.

TEAMS > LONE WOLVES
I used to think the best developers were solo rockstars.
I was wrong.
The best work happens when developers, designers, and stakeholders actually talk to each other.
I ask questions. I share concerns early. I celebrate wins with the team because great products are never built alone.

LEARNING NEVER STOPS
Technology evolves. What was cutting-edge last year is legacy today.
I stay curious, read docs, experiment with new tools, and admit when I don't know something—then go learn it. Ego has no place in development.

MY CREATIVE PROCESS
Honestly? It's messy. I start with coffee and research. I sketch (badly) on paper.
I prototype fast and fail faster. I refactor religiously. I test obsessively. And somewhere in that chaos, something good emerges.

What makes me unique? I care—about the code, the user, the team, and the outcome.
I don't just check boxes; I build solutions that actually solve problems.

Interests & Hobbies

When I'm Not Coding

Plot twist: I have a life outside of VS Code. Shocking, I know.

SIDE PROJECTS & EXPERIMENTS
Okay, so some of my "life outside coding" is... more coding. But hear me out—it's different! These are the projects nobody asked for, the "what if" experiments, the tools I build because I need them and they don't exist yet. My GitHub is a graveyard of brilliant ideas andspectacular failures. No regrets.

PHOTOGRAPHY
I shoot photos. Mostly landscapes, cityscapes, and whatever catches my eye. It started as a way to get away from screens, but I realized composition in photography is weirdly similar to UI design—balance, contrast, negative space, visual hierarchy. Now I can't unsee design principles everywhere.

GAMING (OBVIOUSLY)
I'm a gamer. RPGs, strategy games, indie experiments—I love them all. But I can't play games anymore without analyzing the UI, critiquing load times, and wondering about the backend architecture. Developer brain is a blessing and a curse.

MUSIC IS MY DEBUGGER
I code with music. I think with music. I have playlists for different moods: "Focus Mode" for deep work, "Debug Hell" for when nothing makes sense, and "Deploy Day" for when I'm feeling confident. I also play guitar badly and enjoy every minute of it.

GETTING OUTSIDE
Sitting all day is brutal, so I force myself outside—hiking, biking, walking, whatever.
Some of my best code ideas come when I'm nowhere near a computer. My brain needs to defrag sometimes.

COFFEE CONNOISSEUR (OKAY, ADDICT)
Let's be honest: I run on caffeine. I've explored every coffee shop in my area, I own too many brewing devices, and yes, I have opinions about beans. It's a problem, but I'm not solving it.

Why share this? Because I'm human. I have interests, hobbies, weird obsessions, and a life beyond code. I think better, create better, and work better when I'm not just a developer—I'm a person who happens to develop.
